## 10. Honesty Architecture

Tender Engine AI is built on a principle of transparency we call "Honesty Architecture." This means:

- **Confidence scores are displayed for every extraction stage.** Scores reflect model certainty and are not artificially inflated.
- **Partial-success results remain visible.** If some stages complete successfully while others fail, all outcomes are shown — failures are not hidden.
- **Failed stages remain visible.** When a processing stage fails, that failure is clearly indicated. We do not silently drop failed results.
- **Incomplete results may be returned.** The system may return partial or incomplete results when full processing is not possible.
- **Uncertainty is not hidden.** Stages where the model has low confidence are flagged transparently.

You acknowledge that Honesty Architecture means you will sometimes see failed stages, warnings, low-confidence scores, and incomplete results. This is by design.

## 11. Confidence Scores and Partial-Success Transparency

Every extraction stage in Tender Engine AI reports a confidence score between 0.0 and 1.0. These scores:

- Represent the model's self-assessed certainty for that extraction
- Are not guarantees of accuracy
- May vary based on document quality, format, complexity, and completeness

Processing pipelines run multiple stages (sector detection, duration extraction, location extraction, workforce estimation, BOQ extraction, pricing). Each stage reports independently:

- **Completed:** The stage finished successfully with its assigned confidence score
- **Warning:** The stage completed but with notable caveats or lower confidence
- **Failed:** The stage did not complete successfully

A job may show "partial_success" status, meaning some stages succeeded and some failed. This is a transparency feature, not a defect.
